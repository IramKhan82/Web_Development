Pagani Huayra

The Pagani Huayra, first launched in 2011, was the successor to the legendary Zonda and marked a new era for Pagani.
Powered by a 6.0-liter twin-turbocharged AMG V12 producing around 730 horsepower, it became famous for its cutting-edge active aerodynamics, where flaps on the body adjust in real-time for maximum stability and performance.

Pagani Huayra BC

In 2016, Pagani introduced the Huayra BC, a more extreme and track-focused version dedicated to Benny Caiola, Pagani’s first customer and close friend.
It featured a lighter body thanks to advanced composite materials, along with an upgraded AMG V12 producing nearly 789 horsepower.

Pagani Huayra Roadster BC

Three years later, in 2019, Pagani unveiled the Huayra Roadster BC, an open-top evolution of the BC that combined breathtaking power with an unmatched open-air driving thrill.
Using innovative carbon-titanium materials, it was even lighter and stiffer, while still packing 791 horsepower from the same AMG V12.
